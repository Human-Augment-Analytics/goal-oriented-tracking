# Management & Leadership Mid-Semester Report — Operations Questions

## Define what reoccurring tasks you are doing within your project group.

The following tasks happen on a recurring basis to keep the group organized and moving:

- **Weekly group meeting** - Attending the standing meeting with the full research group to check in on progress, surface any blockers, and align on what is coming up.
- **Meeting notes** - Taking and sharing notes during the weekly meeting so that decisions and action items are written down and easy to reference.
- **Slack monitoring** - Keeping an eye on the group Slack channel throughout the week to stay current on individual researcher updates and any questions that come up between meetings.
- **Weekly progress tracking** - Maintaining a spreadsheet that logs researcher activity and goal progress each week. [View Tracking Sheet](https://gtvault-my.sharepoint.com/:x:/g/personal/mcosta35_gatech_edu/IQCcDIQLPuJVSKWBPgkVgGCkAapcDfmTEzvk963rdX38Ygs?e=auhyic)
- **Weekly initiative meeting with lead researcher** - A one-on-one sync with the lead researcher to review initiative-level progress and make sure goals are on track. *(Just started)*

---

## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project?

The CDDM group is a mature team that is actively working toward publication. My role here is less about setting direction and more about making sure things run smoothly and nothing gets dropped.

The main thing I am watching for is whether I am helping the team move without unnecessary friction. That mostly means staying on top of meetings and communications and making sure nobody gets stuck without a response.

My personal benchmark is simple: if there are no communication or administrative issues that go unresolved for more than a week, I consider my contributions to be positive and on track.

---

## What challenges and successes have you observed in your group?

**Challenges:**

Earlier in the semester, a research direction did not produce the expected results and the team spent a few weeks figuring out how to pivot and keep moving toward publication. That period slowed things down and showed that having clearer goal-level visibility could help the group respond faster when things do not go as planned. This is part of what motivated the goal-first tracking initiative.

**Successes:**

- Communication between researchers has been strong throughout the semester with very few coordination issues.
- Researchers have shown good leadership in managing their own work and keeping things moving.
- The team successfully reassessed their model and changed direction after the initial approach did not work out.
- When the deadline for the first target journal was not met, the group adapted and shifted focus to a different publication rather than stalling out.

---

## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?

Most of the collaboration happens through a few regular touchpoints:

- **One-on-one check-ins** - Regular communication with the lead researcher and/or professor throughout the week to stay aligned and catch any issues early.
- **Weekly group meetings** - Attending the full team meeting each week to stay visible and aware of how the group is doing.
- **Weekly one-on-one with the lead researcher** - A dedicated sync to go over initiative progress and make sure the lead researcher has what they need to keep the team on track.
- **Initiative kickoff meeting** - Led a meeting with the full team to introduce the goal-oriented tracking initiative and walk through the Slack Kanban board setup. This gave everyone a chance to see the system and ask questions before it went live.

---

## What feedback have you received on your role, solicited or unsolicited?

Feedback from the group has been mostly positive. Since the CDDM group already has a well-established workflow, the team has appreciated that I have not come in and tried to change how they operate. Keeping things stable has let them stay focused on their research.

The most specific piece of feedback came during the initiative kickoff meeting. The lead researcher brought up that the existing weekly progress report format, which requires a PDF submission with a set outline, takes too much time relative to what it actually provides. That feedback has been noted. Another Management student is working on a separate initiative to address the reporting process.

---

## What plans do you have for enhancing your role as the project manager with the remaining time in the semester?

For the rest of the semester, the focus will be on two things:

- **Continuing the weekly initiative meetings with the lead researcher** - These just started and I want to build on them to keep initiative progress visible and address any blockers quickly.
- **Tracking velocity toward goals** - Paying closer attention to how fast the team is moving through their goals so that any risks to deadlines can be caught early.

---

## Please provide feedback on what support you may need in your role.

The main thing I would ask for is more interactive, student-driven time during the weekly Management meetings. Having more space for students to bring up topics and lead parts of the discussion would make those sessions more useful and better reflect how research teams actually work.
